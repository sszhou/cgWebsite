What is this qualification?

The School of Design, in association with the School of Engineering and Computer Science, have introduced a new Master's qualification (MDI) in Computer Graphics. Uniting design and computation, this degree blends computer programming and scripting processes with studio approaches to deliver cutting edge computer graphics compositions. The programme was developed in close collaboration with Wellington’s internationally recognised entertainment and digital technology sectors. This relationship with industry leaders such as Weta Digital (Lord of the Rings, Avatar), Sidhe (game development), and Unlimited Realities (advanced interaction design) will be continued in the programme through consultations, guest lectures, and internship opportunities.

What type of degree do you want to pursue?

The Bachelor of Science (BSc) with a focus in Computer Graphics is a three-year degree.

  • YEAR 1
  • DSDN 101 Design Visualisation
  • DSDN 142 Creative Coding
  • COMP 102 Intro to Computer Program Design
  • COMP 103 Intro to Data Structure and Algorithms
  • MATH 161 Discrete Mathematics and Logic
  • MATH 151 Algebra
  • STAT 193 Stats for Natural and Social Sciences
  • YEAR 2
  • MDDN 241 3D Modelling and Animation
  • MDDN 242 Computer Graphics
  • COMP 261 Algorithms and Data Structures
  • and three of:
  • SWEN 200 level courses
  • NWEN 200 level courses
  • YEAR 3
  • COMP 308 Intro to Computer Graphics
  • MDDN 311 Postproduction and Special Effects
  • and three of:
  • COMP 300 level courses
  • SWEN 300 level courses
  • NWEN 300 level courses

Note: Students who do not have 14 NCEA Level 3 credits in English, History, Art History, Classics, Geography or economics will be required to do WRIT 101/1514. And students who do not have 16 Level 3 NCEA credits in Mathematics or equivalent will need to do MATH 132 prior to enrolling in MATH151/161.

The Master of Science (MSc) with a focus in Computer Graphics is a two-year course of study taught in two distinct sections. The MSc comprises 120 points of coursework, 30 points of which are in Design. This is followed by a 120-point thesis focused on Computer Science.

  • YEAR 1
  • COMP 408 Computer Graphics Rendering
  • COMP 409 3D Modelling for Computer Graphics
  • and 60 points from:
  • COMP 400 level courses
  • SWEN 400 level courses
  • NWEN 400 level courses
  • MDDN 400 level courses
  • and one of:
  • MDDN 441 Computer Graphics for Film
  • MDDN 442 Computer Graphics for Interaction Design
  • YEAR 2
  • MDDN 594 Thesis in Computer Graphics

Note: Applicants should have a good first degree in Computer Science, with moderately strong mathematics and sufficient Media Design courses to undertake graduate study in Computer Graphics.

The Bachelor of Design Innovation (BDI) with a focus in Computer Graphics is a three-year degree. The first year of study is shared across all specialisations and introduces students to the fundamentals of design practice and theory in a cross-disciplinary context. However, Media Design students planning for the Masters in Computer Graphics will enrol in a minor in Computer Science, years two and three of the BDI will continue to build the minor with the requisite courses in Computer Science and Software Engineering, in order to systematically build the unique skill set and knowledge base required by this specialisation.

  • YEAR 1
  • DSDN 101 Design Visualisation
  • DSND 111 Ideas and Principles of Design
  • DSDN 171 Design in Context
  • DSDN 112 Introduction to Interaction Design
  • DSDN 142 Creative Coding
  • COMP 102 Intro to Computer Program Design
  • COMP 103 Intro to Data Structure and Algorithms
  • MATH 161 Discrete Mathematics and Logic
  • YEAR 2
  • CCDN 271 Design as Inquiry
  • COMP 261 Algorithms and Data Structures
  • MDDN 242 Computer Graphics
  • MATH 151 Algebra
  • and two of:
  • MDDN 211 Digital Video Creation
  • MDDN 243 Introduction to Computer Game Design
  • MDDN 241 3D Modelling and Animation
  • YEAR 3
  • COMP 308 Intro to Computer Graphics
  • CCDN 331 Live Theory
  • MDDN 311 Postproduction and Special Effects
  • MDDN 343 Advanced Computer Game Design
  • and two of:
  • MDDN 300 level courses

Note: Students who do not have 14 NCEA Level 3 credits in English, History, Art History, Classics, Geography or economics will be required to do WRIT 101/1514. And students who do not have 16 Level 3 NCEA credits in Mathematics or equivalent will need to do MATH 132 prior to enrolling in MATH151/161.

The Master of Design Innovation (MDI) with a focus in Computer Graphics is a two-year course of study taught in two distinct sections. The MDI comprises 150 points of coursework, 30 points of which are in Computer Science. This is followed by a 90-point focused on Design.

  • YEAR 1
  • COMP 408 Computer Graphics Rendering
  • COMP 409 3D Modelling for Computer Graphics
  • DSDN 411 Design Led Futures
  • DSDN 481 Research Methods
  • and one of:
  • MDDN 441 Computer Graphics for Film
  • MDDN 442 Computer Graphics for Interaction Design
  • YEAR 2
  • MDDN 594 Thesis in Computer Graphics
  • and one of:
  • MDDN 441 Computer Graphics for Film
  • MDDN 442 Computer Graphics for Interaction Design
  • DSDN 463 Practicum

Note: Applicants should have a good first degree in Media Design, or the equivalent, with moderately strong technical skills and sufficient Computer Science courses to undertake graduate study in Computer Graphics. A portfolio is required.
